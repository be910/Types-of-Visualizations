# 📊 Visualization Reports
 
A collection of data visualization projects exploring nutrition economics and critical analysis of misleading data presentation in journalism.
 
## Reports
 
### 1. 🥗 Protein Power vs Sugar Shock: The Prepared Meal Showdown

An exploratory visualization report analyzing prepared meal products across major brands to identify which offer the best **protein-to-sugar ratio at the lowest cost** — useful for budget-conscious, health-oriented grocery shopping.
 
**Key Question**
- Which prepared meal brands give you more muscle and less sugar at reasonable prices?
 
An exploratory visualization comparing protein-to-sugar ratios and average prices across the top 5 prepared meal brands: **Great Value, Healthy Choice, Amy's Kitchen, Lean Cuisine, and Whole Foods Market**.

## Report 2 — 🔍 Deceptive Visualization Critique
 
> *Racial Profiling in Action: Black New Yorkers Overrepresented in Police Complaints*
 
A critical analysis of a published visualization on racial disparities in NYPD civilian complaints, examining how specific design choices — intentional or not — shape reader perception of the data.
 
### Key Questions
 
- Does the visualization accurately represent the underlying data?
- What framing, axis, or encoding decisions risk distorting the reader's takeaway?
- How could the same data be presented more transparently?
